Music and Dance

There's always been some interesting discussions regarding dance and how music influences our physical abilities. How scientists are able to see the output of music's effect on our brains and bodies and yet still is unable to determine why such an ethereal and stimulating response can be sparked from sound waves. Similar to that of gravity, we don't know where it comes from nor are we able to pinpoint why, but we do know however the behavior and results. Earth spins, gravity happens. Same with music, our brains are triggered, but it's not one we can explain the reasoning, we only know the why.

Why are dance and music so intertwined? Well that's easier to explain, our emotional triggers are also tied to our natural body movements and interpretation of what we hear. What's difficult for me is to hear the music at all. It has been quite the journey since I've started. Not only could I not hear music, I could not tell you what an up beat or downbeat was. Being a Physicist I dove into the sound waves and theory behind why those waves would trigger such responses in our bodies and the evolution to what we would today call modern dance.

There are many facets of dance. So why would someone be so tied to a specific genre as compared to another? This might be tied into the same reason why humans themselves are created so differently. One book that comes to mind is Signature in the Cell by Stephen Meyer. It ultimately drills down to the key factor that even makes up our chemical composition: DNA. Which is such a fascinating subject in and of itself. Have you ever stopped to look at what DNA actually stands for? Why stop at the acronym? DNA. Deoxyribonucleic Acid. Begging a deeper question, why didn't we ask why in the first place? What is being bread into our innate human curiosity that building inhibition to question?

Why are we not allowed to ask why? What's stopping you from asking why?

When we do ask why, is there a reason for such blockade responses. Abrupt and terse. Did we offend another because we simply are curious? Why did that person take it so, or take it as offending their intelligence?

To ask why should be praised and meaningful. Questions to questions lead to more information, insight, and beautifully carved questions out of human intellect. Without it, we wouldn't have such an insatiable curiosity to do better, do more, and just do.

Music is one of those insatiable stimulants we crave. You think with each passing increment or collection of beats, a rhythm would form. Well not necessarily. Repeated white noise for example. Still sounds, but not pleasing to the ear. This leads me to an original thought from years past around music and its two categorical studies: the art of listening, and the art of composing. Two vastly different categories, but there is a difference. We as listeners do ourselves and the music we are listening to a disservice if we don't study the appreciation of listening and it's complex artistic styling. After all, music is an art of time, which both mathematicians and physicists have studied for millennia.

So why as a musical people and community are we so harsh on those in studies who are drastically diverse in comparison? Math, Physics, Biology, Chemistry, Engineering, Calligraphy, Graphic Design, Medium-Based Art, Technology, Security, Sign Language, Interpretation, Languages, etc. With each category comes specialty and jack of all trades. In technology we call this a T-shaped developer. Specialty in one area where your passion lies, and strong knowledge in remaining categories for a well-rounded engineer and technologist. In Physics we have long believed that individuals do science a disservice when research is not taken from a holistic viewpoint. Do you think there's a correlation with the 21st century and it's lack of true scientific discoveries and breakthroughs? Look back at those who were authored with finding of Deoxyribonucleic Acid: James Watson and Francis Crick. James was a zoologist and Francis was a Physicist and nuero-scientist. Coincidence that our educational system has numbed the human growth by inserting Standardized Testing and Standards of Learning programs? The mark of a great scientist is not the quantity of learned knowledge, it's the capacity for limitless imagination. Sparking imagination comes from teasing one's brain to think outside of the box, not confining to a mold. Same with music.

Dancers and Scientists have much to learn from one another. Dancers feel the music, scientists analyze the sound waves. Yet their goal is the same, demonstrate and express their electro-chemical understanding of vibrations through their physical medium. Celebrate music and learn to love the art of listening and what it has to offer.

Coming from the educational system, I believe as though learning should never come to a close. What's the purpose of anything if there truly is a limit? Immagine what you can learn and then learn even more once you explored the obvious?!? Oh the true joy and happiness one can find through learning; glorious.

Dancers can do the same. And to do the dance, music, expression, and interpretation its best effort should we not do the art justice by learning everything it has to offer and then more? After you've learned music and what it means to you, learn music theory. Then you can really drill into why you were more drawn to certain instruments and sounds as compared to others. Without reasoning and results how can we even do the understanding of art justice? Words and language are both topics with infinite discussions to be had. So put words to your understanding and then build on those blocks to learn more and define what you and your expression means to the music as a dancer and scientist. After all, are scientists and artists so different?

Foundations of dance are science and art. It's the perfect unity! Learn both sides and then take it one step further: teach. This is where you must make a decision. Are you going to be a forever student of dance and mentor those around you through your experiences, or are you willing to take a leap and learn how others learn? This is yet again another categorical study the professional and educational world have been researching since the beginning. How to teach, how to learn, and how to teach so that others learn? All mindsets and understandings are different so how are we supposed to teach what we mean versus what's actually being said? There's the skill. It can be learned to be a good professor of dance, but to be great one must truly learn how others learn. Without that, we do ourselves, our students, and our mentors injustice. Being a great student means being a great learner, and being a great teacher means learning how your students learn. Are you then yourself not a student once again? What an elegant and beautiful cycle!

It is meant to include the best parts of working a remote hybrid, and an office in a mashed an infused way that cross pollinate methodologies to the best use of a software development workshop. Four core principles of Dwow: Self evaluation, Self awareness, Self compartmentalization, Self presentation.

Certain principles are more effective than others, and look to methodically orient best practices that come out of hyper affective sessions. Philosophy on Uniqueness - Blog 0

Our uniqueness is what makes us special, not a conformity to business practices and corporate propaganda. So why not get that chance? Well you have to start somewhere, and it's a lot of work, but with strong community everyone has the opportunity to shine. That's my goal, be a person who can provide a real life example of what I've experienced to provide insight so others have more knowledge going into their own experiences.

This is by no means a billboard posting to say "hey we need to change everything", rather it's a viewpoint on what transitional periods look like, so you can have an insight to take into your ventures.

What's great about millennials (yes there is something great about the entitled culture) is that it teaches us how people want to be satisfied, and how to prevent that in ourselves. Simple reference I use in the tech space: if you can build an app to satisfy a millennial, you've built an app to satisfy all users. It's therefore intuitive, straightforward to use, simplistic in nature, and very servitude based.

So why keep anything from the previous generation? Also a great question. Because you lose experience, knowledge, and learned procedures you'll never once gain again. Ok fair, a person from a newer generation may be more "book learned", if they're reading enough material to formulate and postulate ideas and opinions based on multiple perspectives, but there's one thing the newer generation will never have on the older: experience. Time gives experience, and even someone 10 minutes older than you has more experience on this earth. So regardless of their credentials, give credence to their perspective and learn from it. Einstein himself stated that if you're the smartest person in the room, you're in the wrong room. Which further proves another quote of his: there's no such thing as a fact; there's only experimentally proven theories which can just as quickly be disproved by another theory.

One book I truly recommend is the autobiography of Nikola Tesla. If someone could pack a million thoughts into one sentence it's him. For a publication with less than a few dozen pages, there is a treasure mine of thought put into conciseness that even the elegance of wording proves arabesque and rhythmic in its artistry. He writes in such a way that it's almost as though he knew how to trigger thoughts from the reader and inspire those thoughts to be the readers own. A beautiful quote states: a true scientist is not one of quantified book knowledge, no, rather it's the power to their imagination that makes their possibilities limitless.
