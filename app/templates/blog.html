Digest Way of Working (Dwow, (c) & Restricted) - Blog 1

Dwow is a combination, and re-implementation of agile across a software development workshop. A simple correlation is like Peanut Butter and Jelly. Both materials exist, but the combination, and supplement of new unique materials, provides the consumer with a tremendously more beneficial product. It is meant to include the best parts of working a remote hybrid, and an office in a mashed an infused way that cross pollinate methodologies to the best use of a software development workshop. Four core principles of Dwow: Self evaluation, Self awareness, Self compartmentalization, Self presentation.

Certain principles are more effective than others, and look to methodically oriented, best practices that come out of hyper affective sessions, such as hackathons.

It has been proven that pair programming, swarm sessions, and hackathons are all ways to produce a performing state, and agile that minimizes burnout, and increases productivity , across-the-board. But that being said the ways in which the digital world has enabled us to do so, are expedited, educational programs or learning sessions through a paired style, that we will referred to as paired knowledge sharing. This way, having two keyboards on the same monitor set up, let’s play multiplying effect take place for both users senses. It not only helps the lesser trained individual in this respective field, but also helps the trainer. It helps the trainee by expediting their learning through multiplicative solutions. Multiplication comes from multiple senses being used: the auditory sense by listening to the trainer explain and type at the same time, visual senses by watching solutions, take place and being able to see them in action, the touch sense, because the keyboard in which the trainee is typing also replicate a muscle, memory addition, and Socio economic growth through proximity of a more experienced individual and the subject matter being referenced.

It also helps the trainer because it will force that individual to re-evaluate why thoughts and sequences of solution, and were brought about throughout the problem stage. The solution in stage is rather the more simplistic one in nature, because it is an experimentation phase of the ideation, which was occurring through the problem solving analytical skills of the trainer.

The efficiency of swarm sessions, both code located, and centrally located, are beneficial to all parties in place, including the company with which the sponsored event or session is taking place. This is because not only is it expanded version of the paired knowledge session sharing, but also an advancement of product development and delivery through which natural evolution can not occur at a hyper scale for software applications. Philosophy on Uniqueness - Blog 0

Our uniqueness is what makes us special, not a conformity to business practices and corporate propaganda. So why not get that chance? Well you have to start somewhere, and it's a lot of work, but with strong community everyone has the opportunity to shine. That's my goal, be a person who can provide a real life example of what I've experienced to provide insight so others have more knowledge going into their own experiences.

This is by no means a billboard posting to say "hey we need to change everything", rather it's a viewpoint on what transitional periods look like, so you can have an insight to take into your ventures.

What's great about millennials (yes there is something great about the entitled culture) is that it teaches us how people want to be satisfied, and how to prevent that in ourselves. Simple reference I use in the tech space: if you can build an app to satisfy a millennial, you've built an app to satisfy all users. It's therefore intuitive, straightforward to use, simplistic in nature, and very servitude based.

So why keep anything from the previous generation? Also a great question. Because you lose experience, knowledge, and learned procedures you'll never once gain again. Ok fair, a person from a newer generation may be more "book learned", if they're reading enough material to formulate and postulate ideas and opinions based on multiple perspectives, but there's one thing the newer generation will never have on the older: experience. Time gives experience, and even someone 10 minutes older than you has more experience on this earth. So regardless of their credentials, give credence to their perspective and learn from it. Einstein himself stated that if you're the smartest person in the room, you're in the wrong room. Which further proves another quote of his: there's no such thing as a fact; there's only experimentally proven theories which can just as quickly be disproved by another theory.

One book I truly recommend is the autobiography of Nikola Tesla. If someone could pack a million thoughts into one sentence it's him. For a publication with less than a few dozen pages, there is a treasure mine of thought put into conciseness that even the elegance of wording proves arabesque and rhythmic in its artistry. He writes in such a way that it's almost as though he knew how to trigger thoughts from the reader and inspire those thoughts to be the readers own. A beautiful quote states: a true scientist is not one of quantified book knowledge, no, rather it's the power to their imagination that makes their possibilities limitless.
